2015 San Jose Day of Remembrance on J-Town Community TV
 
If you missed the 2015 San Jose Day of Remembrance event held on February 15, 2015, you can still watch the program on the  "J-Town Community TV" channel.





The 2015 San Jose Day of Remembrance commemorated the signing of Executive Order 9066, which eventually led to the incarceration of 120,000 people of Japanese descent during WW II. Each year, people from diverse communities gather at this event to remember that great civil liberties tragedy and reflect on what that event means to them today.


The program, titled “Stories from the Past, Lessons for Today,” explored personal stories that were passed down by those who were adversely affected by the executive order and the lessons that have been extracted from that experience.

If you have feedback on the 2015 San Jose Day of Remembrance, you can come to the next NOC meeting on Thursday, March 12, 2015 from 7:30 p.m. to 9:00 p.m.  Please contact info@sjnoc.org for location information.

The Day of Remembrance is an event that aims to bring different communities together in order to build trust, respect, and understanding among all people and to renew our pledge to fight for equality, justice, and peace.

Congressional
Gold Medal Dinner

Friday, June 12, 2015

A special fundraising dinner, organized to support the Congressional Gold Medal Digital Exhibition which highlights the stories of the World War II Nisei soldiers, will be held on Friday, June 12, at the Fairmont Hotel in San Jose. The event will feature a special thank you to former U.S. Secretary of Veterans Affairs Eric Shinseki.
The digital exhibition is being developed by the National Veterans Network (NVN) in collaboration with the Smithsonian Asian Pacific American Center and the National Museum of American History and will be unveiled at the Smithsonian in 2016.

To purchase tickets, sponsor veterans, or leave a thank you message, please visit
