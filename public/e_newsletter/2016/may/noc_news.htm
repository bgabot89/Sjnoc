2nd Annual J-Town Film Fest San Jose, May 20-22, 2016
J-Town Community TV: 2016 Day of Remembrance highlights
 
 
During  the 1980s,  NOC played an important role in building support within the San Jose community for Japanese American redress and the passage of the Civil Liberties Act of 1988.

NOC is proud to be a presenter of the J-Town Film Fest film, Right of Passage. The film sheds new light on the struggle for redress with recently declassified documents and dozens of interviews with individuals who were directly involved in the legislative process.

The first-ever San Jose screening of  this film will be followed by a conversation with director Janice Tanaka, producer Nancy Araki, and former Civil Liberties Public Education Fund vice-chair and former NOC chairperson Susan Hayase.

The film festival will also present another interesting film, Resistance at Tule Lake, which is a film that is currently being prepared for public broadcasting. The film documents the turmoil and unrest at the Tule Lake Segregation Center and challenges the viewer on the concepts of loyalty, patriotism, and the role of dissent in a democracy.
